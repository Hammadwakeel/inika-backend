Where Is Coorg Located and Why Its Weather Is Unique
Coorg is located in the Kodagu district of Karnataka in southern India. It lies within the famous Western Ghats mountain range, one of the world’s biodiversity hotspots. The region sits at an elevation of roughly
1,150 to 1,450 meters above sea level
, which plays a major role in shaping its cool and pleasant climate.
Unlike nearby cities such as Bangalore and Mysore, which experience warmer temperatures during much of the year, Coorg enjoys milder weather due to its altitude and dense forest cover. The area is surrounded by thick forests, sprawling coffee plantations, spice farms, and misty hills that help maintain a naturally cooler atmosphere.
The abundance of greenery also contributes to the region’s fresh air and moderate temperatures. Trees and vegetation absorb heat and release moisture into the atmosphere, which helps keep the environment cool even during warmer months.
Because of this unique geography, Coorg rarely experiences extreme temperatures. Summers remain comfortable compared to most parts of South India, while winters are cool but not harsh. The monsoon season brings heavy rainfall that transforms the region into a vibrant green paradise with flowing waterfalls and misty valleys.
This combination of
high elevation, dense forests, and plantation landscapes
creates the refreshing climate that makes Coorg such a beloved destination for nature lovers and travelers seeking a relaxing escape.
