Introduction
Nestled in the Western Ghats of Karnataka, Coorg, also known as Kodagu, is often referred to as the “
Scotland of India
” for its rolling hills, lush green landscapes, and scenic beauty. A visit to Coorg is always special, but there’s something uniquely enchanting about experiencing this hill station in May. Pre-monsoon showers start to sprinkle the region, enhancing the greenery and adding a touch of mist to the hills. The weather is warm but not yet saturated with the monsoon, making it an ideal time for travelers seeking serenity, natural beauty, and adventure.
May is also the shoulder season in Coorg, meaning fewer crowds compared to the peak tourist months of October to March. For nature lovers, photographers, trekkers, and anyone looking to escape city life, Coorg in May provides a perfect blend of adventure and tranquility. From exploring coffee plantations and waterfalls to enjoying misty mornings at scenic viewpoints, this guide will take you through everything you need to know for a memorable May trip to Coorg.

Weather in Coorg in May
Understanding Coorg’s weather in May is crucial for planning your trip effectively. The average temperature during the day ranges between
28°C to 32°C
, while nights remain cooler at around
20°C to 22°C
. Humidity levels begin to rise as the monsoon approaches, which can make the afternoons feel warmer. However, mornings and evenings are pleasant, offering the perfect time for outdoor activities.
Pre-monsoon showers are common in May, often brief and refreshing. These showers rejuvenate the region’s flora, making waterfalls, rivers, and forests look even more vibrant. Light rains also reduce the dust on trekking trails and plantation paths, creating a cleaner, more enjoyable experience for visitors.

for daytime exploration.
Keep a
raincoat or umbrella
handy for sudden showers.
Wear
comfortable shoes
suitable for trekking or walking on wet paths.
Visiting Coorg in May allows you to enjoy the green landscapes before the monsoon fully sets in, creating a perfect balance between adventure and comfort.
Why Visit Coorg in May
Coorg in May has a charm that is distinct from other months. The reasons to visit during this time include:
1. Lush Green Landscapes
The pre-monsoon showers start painting the hills and valleys in vivid green hues. Coffee plantations, paddy fields, and forests become even more picturesque, offering excellent photography opportunities.
2. Fewer Tourists
Compared to the peak winter months, May sees fewer travelers. This means quieter tourist spots, less crowded waterfalls, and more peaceful trekking trails. You get to enjoy the serenity of Coorg without the hustle and bustle of high season tourism.
3. Wildlife Sightings
The region’s wildlife begins to emerge more visibly during May. You may spot elephants, deer, and various bird species near forest edges or riverbanks. Birdwatchers find this season particularly rewarding as migratory species are sometimes spotted.
4. Adventure Opportunities
May is ideal for moderate trekking and hiking as trails are mostly dry before the heavy monsoon sets in. Rivers are flowing, but not at dangerous levels, making river walks or small-scale adventure activities safer.
Best Places to Visit in Coorg in May
Coorg has a variety of attractions to explore in May, ranging from waterfalls and coffee plantations to hills, wildlife sanctuaries, and cultural sites.
1. Waterfalls
Waterfalls in Coorg are spectacular in May due to the pre-monsoon rains, which increase water flow slightly but not dangerously. Key waterfalls include:
Abbey Falls
– Surrounded by lush greenery and coffee plantations, Abbey Falls is a must-visit. A short trek from the parking area leads to a viewpoint where you can capture the cascading water framed by trees.
Iruppu Falls
– Located on the Brahmagiri Range, Iruppu Falls is perfect for a day trip. The flow of water in May is moderate, offering safe but spectacular views.
Tips for Visiting Waterfalls:
Wear
non-slip footwear
for wet areas.
Avoid swimming or wading near strong currents.
Visit early morning or late afternoon for the best lighting for photography.
2. Coffee Plantations
Coorg is famous for its aromatic coffee plantations, which reach their peak beauty in May. Guided plantation walks provide insights into the coffee-growing process, including pruning and soil preparation before the monsoon. Walking through the neatly lined coffee bushes in the fresh morning air is an experience every visitor cherishes.
3. Hills and Viewpoints
Coorg’s hills offer panoramic views of valleys, plantations, and the surrounding Western Ghats.
Raja’s Seat
– Known for breathtaking sunrise and sunset views. In May, mornings are misty, making the views even more magical.
Tadiandamol Peak
– The highest peak in Coorg, perfect for trekking enthusiasts. The pre-monsoon conditions make the climb safer and more scenic.
4. Wildlife and Nature Spots
Nagarhole outskirts & Dubare Forest
– Spot elephants, birds, and other wildlife during early morning or late evening walks.
Nature Trails
– Explore misty forest paths with lush vegetation rejuvenated by May rains.
Activities to Enjoy in May
May offers a range of activities that let you enjoy Coorg’s natural beauty and adventurous spirit:
1. Trekking and Hiking
Pre-monsoon trails are ideal for trekking. Popular treks like Tadiandamol, Brahmagiri, and Pushpagiri offer moderate challenges and breathtaking views.
2. River Walks & Rafting
Smaller rivers and streams provide opportunities for gentle river walks. Though river rafting may be limited in May, the flowing rivers are perfect for light adventure and photography.
3. Photography
The lush greenery, misty mornings, and vibrant flora make May perfect for photographers. Capture landscapes, waterfalls, and plantation life without the distraction of large crowds.
4. Cultural Exploration
Explore local Kodava culture through traditional villages, temples, and community interactions. May is also the time when local festivals or harvest preparations may be taking place in rural areas.
Travel Tips for May
To make your trip comfortable and safe, keep the following tips in mind:
Best time of day for sightseeing
: Early morning for waterfalls, wildlife, and plantations; late afternoon for viewpoints and photography.
Clothing essentials
: Light cotton clothes, comfortable trekking shoes, hats, sunglasses, and a raincoat or umbrella.
Stay hydrated
: Carry water bottles and light snacks, especially during treks.
Transport
: Self-drive vehicles or hired cabs offer flexibility for exploring multiple spots in a day.
Accommodation Advice
Coorg offers a range of accommodation options, from hillside cottages and eco-lodges to homestays surrounded by nature. Staying close to plantations or forest areas allows travelers to experience the natural serenity of Coorg in May. Opt for places with good ventilation, shaded areas, and easy access to nearby attractions.
Tip:
Booking early is advisable, even in the shoulder season, to secure homes with scenic views or unique natural surroundings.
Food and Local Cuisine
Coorg’s cuisine is rich in flavors and reflects the Kodava culture. May is an excellent time to try seasonal fruits like mangoes, jackfruit, and custard apple. Traditional Kodava dishes include:
Pandi Curry
– Spicy pork curry with local spices.
Kadambuttu
– Steamed rice dumplings, often paired with curry.
Local Coffee
– Savor freshly brewed coffee grown in nearby plantations.
Sampling local dishes enhances the immersive experience of a May visit to Coorg.

Safety and Health Tips
Pre-monsoon heat can be intense; avoid prolonged exposure during midday.
Carry basic first aid supplies for minor cuts, scrapes, or insect bites.
Follow wildlife safety rules and maintain a safe distance from animals.
Be cautious while trekking in wet or slippery areas.
FAQs about Visiting Coorg in May
Q1: Is Coorg too hot in May?
Not really. While daytime temperatures can reach 32°C, mornings and evenings are cooler, and pre-monsoon showers make the climate comfortable.
Q2: Can I trek in Coorg during May?
Yes, trekking is ideal before heavy monsoon rains. Moderate trails are safe and scenic.
Q3: What should I pack for Coorg in May?
Pack light cotton clothing, a hat, sunglasses, raincoat, comfortable shoes, and essential toiletries.
Q4: Are waterfalls safe to visit in May?
Yes, pre-monsoon flow is moderate. Exercise caution near strong currents and slippery rocks.
