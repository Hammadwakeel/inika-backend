21 Best Places to Visit in Coorg Madikeri – A Complete Travel Guide
Coorg, officially known as Kodagu, is one of the most scenic hill regions in Karnataka and a true retreat for nature lovers. Nestled in the Western Ghats, Coorg is shaped by rolling hills, dense evergreen forests, winding rivers, and vast stretches of coffee plantations that give the region its distinctive charm. The climate remains pleasant for most of the year, with cool mornings, misty evenings, and refreshing mountain air.
Madikeri, the heart of Coorg, is located at an elevation of around 1,450 meters above sea level and acts as the main gateway for travelers exploring the region. From here, scenic roads lead to waterfalls, viewpoints, temples, trekking trails, and quiet villages that showcase Coorg’s natural and cultural richness. The region is also known for its biodiversity, offering opportunities for birdwatching, forest walks, and wildlife experiences.
Often referred to as the “Scotland of India,” Coorg is ideal for travelers seeking slow travel, scenic beauty, and peaceful surroundings rather than crowded sightseeing. The region appeals equally to couples, families, and solo travelers who enjoy unhurried days, nature-based activities, and authentic local experiences.

1. Abbey Falls
Abbey Falls is one of the most visited natural attractions in Coorg and is located a short drive from Madikeri town. The waterfall flows through dense greenery, coffee plantations, and spice estates, creating a dramatic and refreshing setting. Water cascades down rugged rocks into a pool below, producing a loud yet soothing sound that adds to the experience. A hanging bridge near the falls allows visitors to enjoy panoramic views and take photographs safely. The surrounding forest paths make the walk to the falls enjoyable. Abbey Falls looks especially beautiful after the monsoon when water flow is strong and the landscape is lush and green.
2. Chiklihole Reservoir
Chiklihole Reservoir is a peaceful and lesser-known destination in Coorg, located between Madikeri and Kushalnagar. Unlike crowded tourist spots, this reservoir offers quiet surroundings, open skies, and scenic views of still water bordered by forested hills. It is an ideal place for travelers who enjoy solitude, nature photography, or relaxed picnics. Sunsets here are particularly beautiful, with changing colors reflecting on the water surface. Since there are no shops or facilities nearby, visitors should carry water and snacks. The calm atmosphere makes it a great stop for families and nature lovers seeking slow, uninterrupted time in nature.
3. Dubare Elephant Camp
Dubare Elephant Camp is located on the banks of the Cauvery River and is a popular destination for wildlife and nature enthusiasts. The camp allows visitors to observe elephants up close while learning about their daily routines, food habits, and conservation efforts. Visitors can watch trained caretakers bathe and feed elephants in the river, which is a memorable experience, especially for children. The surrounding forest and river landscape adds to the charm of the place. Apart from elephants, the area is also rich in birdlife. Dubare is best visited in the morning hours when most activities take place.
4. Harangi Dam
Harangi Dam is built across a tributary of the Cauvery River and is one of the earliest dams in the region. The dam offers wide views of water spread across a scenic valley, especially during the monsoon and post-monsoon seasons when water levels are high. Cool breezes and open surroundings make it a pleasant spot for short visits and picnics. Compared to other attractions, Harangi Dam is relatively less crowded, making it suitable for travelers who prefer quiet places. The gentle sound of flowing water and surrounding greenery create a relaxing environment ideal for unwinding during a Coorg trip.
5. Honnamana Kere Lake
Honnamana Kere is the largest lake in Coorg and holds both scenic and cultural significance. Surrounded by hills, forests, and coffee plantations, the lake offers a peaceful atmosphere for visitors. The calm water and open surroundings make it suitable for boating, fishing, and relaxed walks along the shore. Nearby, a temple dedicated to Goddess Honnamma attracts devotees, especially during festivals, adding cultural depth to the visit. The area around the lake is ideal for photography, nature appreciation, and quiet reflection. Honnamana Kere is a great stop for travelers interested in combining natural beauty with local traditions.
6. Omkareswara Temple
Omkareswara Temple is a historic Shiva temple located in Madikeri and is known for its unique architectural style. Built in the early 19th century, the temple blends Gothic, Islamic, and traditional elements, making it visually distinct from typical South Indian temples. A small water tank in front of the temple adds to its peaceful setting. The temple complex is calm and well-maintained, offering a quiet space for prayer and reflection. Visitors often stop here while exploring Madikeri town, as it is close to other attractions. The serene atmosphere makes it suitable even for those seeking spiritual calm rather than religious rituals.
7. Kotebetta Peak
Kotebetta is one of the higher peaks in Coorg and is popular among trekking enthusiasts and nature lovers. The trek to the summit passes through coffee plantations, forest paths, and open grasslands, offering scenic views throughout the journey. The climb is moderately challenging and rewards trekkers with panoramic views of surrounding hills and valleys. A small temple at the peak adds cultural significance to the location. Kotebetta is less crowded compared to other trekking spots, making it ideal for those seeking a peaceful hiking experience. The best time to trek is during cooler months when visibility is clear and weather is pleasant.
8. Madikeri Fort
Madikeri Fort is a historic landmark that reflects the rich and layered history of Coorg. Originally built centuries ago, the fort has undergone several reconstructions by different rulers. Today, it houses a museum, a temple, and a church within its complex. Visitors can explore stone walls, old structures, and exhibits that provide insights into the region’s past. The fort is centrally located in Madikeri town, making it easy to visit. It also offers elevated views of the surrounding area. Madikeri Fort is ideal for travelers interested in history, architecture, and cultural exploration during their Coorg visit.
9. Mallalli Falls
Mallalli Falls is one of the tallest waterfalls in Coorg and is located at the base of the Pushpagiri hill range. The waterfall is formed by the Kumaradhara River cascading down steep rock faces, creating a powerful and scenic sight. During the monsoon season, the falls are at their most impressive, with strong water flow and lush greenery all around. Reaching the base requires a short walk, adding a sense of adventure to the visit. The area is surrounded by forested hills, making it a great spot for nature lovers and photographers seeking dramatic landscapes and fresh mountain air.
10. Mandalpatti Viewpoint
Mandalpatti is a popular hilltop viewpoint known for its misty landscapes and sweeping views of Coorg’s rolling hills. Located a short distance from Madikeri, reaching Mandalpatti usually involves a jeep ride or a trek, adding excitement to the journey. The viewpoint offers open views of valleys, forests, and distant mountains, making it a favorite spot for sunrise and sunset visits. The atmosphere is calm, with cool winds and clouds often drifting across the hills. Mandalpatti is ideal for travelers looking to experience Coorg’s natural beauty from a high vantage point without heavy tourist crowds.
11. Nalknad Palace
Nalknad Palace is a historic structure located near the base of Tadiandamol Peak, surrounded by dense forests and rolling hills. Built in the late 18th century, the palace served as a safe retreat for Coorg’s royal family during times of conflict. Though simple in appearance, it holds great historical importance and offers insight into the region’s past. The palace has thick stone walls, inner chambers, and ancient corridors that reflect traditional architecture. Today, it is also used as a base point for trekkers heading toward Tadiandamol Peak. The calm surroundings, cool climate, and forested views make it a peaceful heritage stop. Visitors often combine this visit with trekking or nature walks. It is ideal for history lovers who enjoy quiet places rather than crowded tourist attractions.
12. Nehru Mantap
Nehru Mantap is a quiet and lesser-known viewpoint located close to Raja’s Seat in Madikeri. Unlike more crowded spots, this location offers silence, open views, and a relaxed atmosphere. From the viewpoint, visitors can enjoy sweeping views of Madikeri town, surrounding hills, and distant valleys. The area is shaded by trees and often visited by those seeking solitude, meditation, or peaceful photography. Sunrise and sunset are especially beautiful here, with soft light spreading across the hills. The short walk to reach the mantap adds to its charm without being physically demanding. Nehru Mantap is perfect for travelers who want to slow down and enjoy Coorg’s natural beauty without distractions. It is also a favorite spot for artists and writers who appreciate calm environments and inspiring views.
13. Padi Iggutappa Temple
Padi Iggutappa Temple is one of the most culturally significant temples in Coorg and is located near Virajpet, surrounded by dense forests and hills. Dedicated to Lord Iggutappa, the temple holds deep importance for local traditions and festivals. The architecture reflects traditional styles, blending naturally with its forest setting. The peaceful surroundings and fresh mountain air make it a calm place for reflection, even for visitors who are not religious. Annual festivals attract devotees from across the region, bringing the area to life with rituals and celebrations. Outside festival times, the temple remains quiet and serene. The scenic drive to reach the temple adds to the experience, passing through coffee plantations and forest roads. It is a meaningful stop for those interested in culture, spirituality, and the traditional heritage of Coorg.
14. Raja’s Seat
Raja’s Seat is one of the most popular viewpoints in Madikeri and is known for its breathtaking valley views. Historically, it was a favorite relaxation spot for Coorg’s kings, who came here to enjoy sunrise and sunset views. Today, the area is beautifully landscaped with gardens, walking paths, and seating areas. Visitors can enjoy panoramic views of mist-covered hills, lush valleys, and changing skies, especially during the evening. A small park nearby makes it suitable for families, children, and elderly travelers. The viewpoint is easily accessible and well-maintained, making it a must-visit stop in Madikeri. Raja’s Seat offers a perfect balance of natural beauty and comfort, allowing visitors to enjoy Coorg’s landscapes without long walks or treks.
15. Somwarpet
Somwarpet is a quieter town in Coorg that offers a slower pace compared to popular tourist hubs. Surrounded by lakes, hills, forests, and coffee plantations, it is ideal for travelers seeking natural beauty without heavy crowds. The town is close to several scenic spots, including lakes and wildlife areas, making it a good base for nature exploration. Somwarpet also reflects everyday local life, allowing visitors to experience Coorg beyond tourist attractions. Early mornings are especially peaceful, with misty views and cool weather. The surrounding countryside is perfect for walks, photography, and birdwatching. Somwarpet is well-suited for travelers who prefer calm environments and authentic experiences rather than busy sightseeing schedules.
16. Suntikoppa
Suntikoppa is a scenic town located along the road connecting Madikeri and Kushalnagar. Known for its coffee estates and green landscapes, it offers beautiful drives and refreshing views. The area is popular among nature lovers and birdwatchers due to its rich plant and bird life. Suntikoppa also serves as a pleasant stop for travelers exploring Coorg by road, offering small eateries and quiet viewpoints. The region feels relaxed and uncrowded, making it suitable for short breaks or overnight stays. Gentle hills, cool climate, and plantation surroundings make it a calming place to visit. Suntikoppa is ideal for travelers who enjoy scenic routes, countryside charm, and simple moments surrounded by nature.
17. Tadiandamol Peak
Tadiandamol Peak is the highest peak in Coorg and one of the most rewarding trekking destinations in the region. The trek takes visitors through forests, streams, grasslands, and open mountain slopes. Though challenging, the trail offers stunning views at every stage, making the effort worthwhile. The summit provides panoramic views of Coorg’s hills and valleys, often covered in mist and clouds. The best time for trekking is during cooler months when visibility is clear. Tadiandamol is best suited for experienced trekkers or physically active travelers. The peaceful environment and natural beauty make it a memorable adventure for those seeking a deeper connection with Coorg’s landscapes.
18. Talakaveri
Talakaveri is considered the origin of the Cauvery River and is located in the Brahmagiri hills. The site holds great spiritual importance and is visited by pilgrims throughout the year. Apart from its religious value, Talakaveri also offers scenic views of misty hills and open landscapes. The cool climate and fresh air add to the peaceful atmosphere. Nearby viewpoints allow visitors to enjoy panoramic views of the surrounding region. The area is well-maintained and accessible by road, making it suitable for families and elderly travelers. Talakaveri combines natural beauty with spiritual significance, offering a calm and meaningful experience for visitors.
19. Yemmemadu Dargah
Yemmemadu Dargah is a serene Sufi shrine located in a quiet rural area of Coorg. Surrounded by greenery and open land, the dargah reflects the region’s cultural harmony and spiritual diversity. Visitors come here seeking peace, reflection, and a break from busy travel schedules. The atmosphere is calm and respectful, making it suitable for quiet contemplation. Annual gatherings draw larger crowds, but outside these times, the area remains tranquil. The surrounding countryside adds to the peaceful experience, with gentle landscapes and rural charm. Yemmemadu Dargah is ideal for travelers interested in spiritual spaces and cultural heritage.
20. Karada Village
Karada is a traditional village near Virajpet known for its coffee plantations, ancestral homes, and rural lifestyle. The village offers an authentic glimpse into everyday life in Coorg, away from tourist-focused areas. Old houses, plantation roads, and quiet surroundings define the village’s charm. Visitors can enjoy slow walks, interact with locals, and observe traditional farming practices. The area is also suitable for nature walks and photography, with scenic views of estates and hills. Karada is perfect for travelers seeking simplicity, culture, and calm environments that reflect Coorg’s roots rather than its tourist image.
21. Gaddige Raja’s Tomb
Gaddige, also known as Raja’s Tomb, is a historic site located on a hill overlooking Madikeri. The tombs belong to former rulers and feature a unique blend of architectural styles. Set amidst gardens and greenery, the location offers peaceful surroundings and elevated views of the town below. The structure’s domes and stonework reflect the craftsmanship of its time. Visitors often come here to explore history while enjoying quiet walks and scenic viewpoints. Gaddige is less crowded compared to other attractions, making it suitable for relaxed exploration. It is an ideal stop for history enthusiasts and travelers who enjoy combining heritage with calm natural settings.
